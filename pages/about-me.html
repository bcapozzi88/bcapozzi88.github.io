About Me

I have a background primarily in physics, and I am currently in the 5th year of a PhD program in Applied Physics at Columbia University in New York City. My research group focuses on the field of molecular electronics, looking to understand the fundamentals of current flow through nanometer-scale circuits composed of single molecules. My research has examined how one can use the effects of environmental conditions about such molecular circuits to create single-molecule analogues of classical circuit elements, such as diodes and transistors. The work is highly interdisciplinary and very collaborative. My group works closely with synthetic chemists, theoretical chemists, and computational physicists to gain insight into nanoscale phenomena, and I have learned a lot from collaborating with people from such different backgrounds.

I have recently become interested in the fields of data science and machine learning, hence, this blog. Feel free to contact me with any questions or comments at bc2360@columbia.edu. I'd be happy to receive any feedback!
